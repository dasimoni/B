3D · interactive

Brain in 3D

The whole thing in three dimensions — two cortical hemispheres you can orbit and zoom, the sense organs in space, relays and spinal cord, and every wire (sensory, callosum, cortico-cortical, feedback) as 3D tubes.

  • Drag to rotate · scroll to zoom · hover to inspect
  • Toggle each wiring layer on/off
  • Built with Three.js (needs an internet connection)

The Unfolded Cortex

Each hemisphere as a continuous parcellated sheet with smooth, sulcus-like borders where every region's area is proportional to its real unfolded cm² (V1 big, A1 tiny, prefrontal dominant), plus the sensory relay stations at true relative size and one fiber-count → width standard.

  • Smoothly-bordered regions sized by measured cortical surface area
  • Thalamic nuclei (LGN unfolded into 6 layers, MGN, pulvinar, MD…), colliculi & olfactory bulb at true relative volume
  • One width standard for every wire; feed-forward vs feedback in color

The Unfolded Brain

The brain's wiring laid flat as a layered network — sensory input at the top, motor output at the bottom — with crossings minimized so it reads cleanly.

  • 34 regions across 10 functional layers
  • Named white-matter tracts (arcuate, Papez, corticospinal…)
  • Best for understanding the overall flow

Anatomical · real counts

Brain Fiber Circuit

Real named nerves traced from the sense organs inward, where line thickness is the measured number of fibers — vision's million-fiber cable vs. hearing's thirty-thousand.

  • Cited histological fiber counts only
  • Optic ~1M · cochlear ~30k · callosum ~200M
  • Best for grasping the real scale of each nerve
